An Integrated AI-Driven Decision Support System for Adaptive Patient Monitoring and Autonomous Clinic Workflow Optimization
1.	Technical Architecture & Stack
The system follows an Edge-to-Cloud Intelligence model. Monitoring is handled at the "Edge" for zero-latency alerts, while workflow optimization is processed in the "Cloud."

Layer				Technology					Role in the System
Edge Monitoring		ESP32 / Raspberry Pi		Interfaces with wearable/bedside sensors (SpO2, Heart Rate, Temp).
Communication		HL7 / FHIR Standards		Ensures healthcare data interoperability and secure exchange.
AI/ML Layer			PyTorch / Scikit- learn		Powers the Early Warning System (EWS) and Queueing Theory models.
Backend				FastAPI (Python)			High-concurrency API for clinical data and logic execution.
Database			TimescaleDB (PostgreSQL)	Optimized for time-series vitals and longitudinal patient records.
Frontend			Next.js 15 (PWA)			Real-time Nurse/Doctor dashboard with low- latency updates.

2. Core Functional Modules

Module A: Adaptive Patient Monitoring
	•	Early Warning System (EWS): AI calculates a "Vital Score" in real-time. If multiple vitals trend toward instability, it triggers a "Code" alert before a crisis occurs.
	•	Context-Aware Alarms: Reduces "Alarm Fatigue" by using AI to distinguish between a sensor being disconnected and an actual physiological event.
	•	Longitudinal Vitals Mapping: Visualizes patient trends over days or weeks to detect subtle recovery or decline patterns.

Module B: Autonomous Clinic Workflow Optimization
	•	Smart Triage & Queueing: AI analyzes patient symptoms and physician availability to dynamically re-order the clinic queue for maximum efficiency.
	•	Resource Allocation: Predicts peak patient hours and suggests staff shifts or room assignments to prevent bottlenecks.
	•	Automated Documentation: (Optional integration) Speech-to-text AI to assist in updating Electronic Health Records (EHR) during consultations.

Module C: Integrated Decision Support System (DSS)
	•	Clinical Pathway Suggestion: Based on patient vitals and history, the DSS suggests evidence-based treatment protocols.
	•	Medication Interaction Alert: Cross-references new prescriptions with current patient data to prevent adverse drug events.

Module D: Command Center Dashboard
	•	Clinic Heatmap: Visualizes room occupancy, wait times, and patient acuity levels across the facility.
	•	Device Health Monitor: Tracks the battery and connectivity status of all bedside IoT monitoring nodes.

3. Interface Design Outline (Sitemap)
	1. Clinical Ward Overview (Nurse/MD View)
		•	Patient Grid: High-level cards for every patient showing [Name | Bed # | Real-time Vitals | Risk Status].
		•	Alert Pane: Red-highlighted sidebar for active emergency triggers.
		•	Trend View: Expandable charts for SpO2, Heart Rate, and Blood Pressure.
	2. Workflow & Queue Management
		•	Live Queue: Dynamic list of patients in the waiting area with AI-calculated "Priority Scores."
		•	Staffing Board: Visual representation of available doctors and assigned consultation rooms.
		•	[Form] Manual Triage Override: Allows staff to manually move a patient to the top of the queue if needed.
	3.Patient Record & Monitoring Setup
		•	[Form] Sensor Pair: Interface to link a specific IoT device ID to a Patient ID.
		•	Vital Thresholds: Custom sliders to set "Normal" ranges for specific patient conditions.

4. System Flow Guide (The Developer’s Logic)
	1.Phase 1 (Data Capture): Bedside IoT sensors capture vitals and transmit them via HL7/FHIR protocols to the backend.
	2.Phase 2 (Risk Evaluation): The Adaptive AI compares the vitals against the patient's baseline.
	3.Phase 3 (Alerting): If the AI detects a "Respiratory Distress" pattern, an instant WebSocket notification is pushed to the PWA on the nurse's tablet.
	4.Phase 4 (Workflow Adjustment): Simultaneously, the Workflow AI detects the emergency and automatically marks the assigned doctor as "Busy," updating the waiting room queue estimated times.
	5.Phase 5 (Documentation): The event and the vitals at the time of the alert are automatically logged into the TimescaleDB for audit.
	6.Phase 6 (Intervention): The staff responds; the vitals stabilize; the AI clears the alert and resumes normal monitoring.

5.Security, Ethics, & Reliability
	•	HIPAA/Data Privacy Compliance: All patient-identifiable information (PII) is encrypted (AES-256) at rest and in transit (TLS 1.3).
	•	Fail-Safe Mechanism: In the event of a Wi-Fi failure, the Edge devices must have local audible alarms to ensure patient safety.
	•	AI Explainability: Decisions made by the DSS (like triage priority) must be accompanied by a "Reasoning" tag (e.g., "Priority: High - Abnormal Tachycardia Detected").
